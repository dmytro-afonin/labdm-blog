## Everything was changing

It was 2025. AI was everywhere — but not for me, not for my dev friends, not for the company where I work. So for me it felt like AI was still too far away from programming. Boy, was I wrong. My friend, a designer from game dev, told me every day that I should learn stuff, but I did not get it: he works with images, and his pipelines and tools are completely different from code. He could not convince me at all — until the day I tried Copilot. I realized it had a free tier and asked it to write a unit test. And then it clicked! I started saying everywhere we need subs, we need AI. I got some traction — we got Copilot licenses and I was hooked. Then Sonnet 3.5, Sonnet 4.5, 4.6. Right now it is frustratingly hard to imagine writing code by hand — I refuse to do it even for a small change.

## Why a blog

So how does all of this relate to me writing this blog? AI changed a lot. I was burned out — now I am inspired again. I am just not sure how long that will last. Yes, I can do fewer chores in the project. But now that I focus on process, business, and technical decisions more than ever — I realize the future feels unstable for me. AI is replacing devs fast, and companies are doing weird things. I started to worry: what happens if I am laid off suddenly? Will I find a new job? How do I make a résumé and pass an interview now, in the era of résumé slop and AI interview cheaters? I cannot rely on HR randomly picking me over some student who just learned how to prompt and is showcasing his video‑coded slop. So I thought maybe it is time to go public — to show who I am and what I am interested in. Start talking to other devs and become visible in the community. There is no better place to start than building your own things, so I am starting with a blog…
